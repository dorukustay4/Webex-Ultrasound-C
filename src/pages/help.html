Chapter 1: Your First Annotation Session

Let's create your first annotation session from start to finish. Follow these detailed steps:

  1. 1
    Launch the Application
    Double-click the application icon or run the executable file. The system will initialize and show the main dashboard. Wait for the "Ready" indicator in the bottom status bar.
  2. 2
    Navigate to Home Dashboard
    If not already there, click "Home" in the left sidebar. You'll see the main dashboard with session statistics and the prominent "Start Session" button.
  3. 3
    Prepare Your Images
    Before starting, organize your ultrasound images in a folder. Supported formats include DICOM (.dcm), JPEG (.jpg), PNG (.png), and TIFF (.tiff). Ensure images are of good quality and properly labeled.
  4. 4
    Click "Start Session"
    The large blue button will take you to the session setup page. This is where you'll configure your annotation session parameters.
  5. 5
    Upload Your Images
    Click "Upload Images" or drag files directly to the upload area. You can select multiple files at once. The system will show upload progress and validate each image format.
  6. 6
    Configure Session Details
    Fill in the required information:
    • Doctor Name: Your name or the annotating physician
    • Session Category: Choose from nerve blocks, vascular access, or custom
    • Patient Demographics: Age group and relevant clinical context
    • Notes: Any additional context about the image set
  7. 7
    Enter Annotation Workspace
    Click "Begin Annotation" to enter the main annotation interface. The first image will load automatically, and annotation tools will be available on the left toolbar.
First-Timer Tip: Start with just 2-3 high-quality images for your first session. This helps you learn the tools without feeling overwhelmed.

Chapter 2: Understanding the Annotation Interface

Once in the annotation workspace, you'll see several key areas. Let's explore each one:

Main Image Area

The central workspace where your ultrasound image appears.

  • Images are automatically scaled to fit the viewport
  • Use mouse wheel to zoom in/out for detail work
  • Click and drag to pan around large images
  • Double-click to reset to original view

Tool Palette (Left Side)

Contains all annotation tools and controls.

  • Selection Tool: Default tool for selecting existing annotations
  • Polygon Tool: Primary tool for tracing nerve boundaries
  • Pan Tool: For moving around the image
  • Zoom Tools: Magnifying glass icons for zoom control

Properties Panel (Right Side)

Shows details and allows editing of selected annotations.

  • Medical attributes for each annotation
  • Coordinate information and area measurements
  • Color and visibility settings
  • Clinical notes and observations

Image Navigator (Bottom)

Thumbnail strip for multi-image sessions.

  • Click thumbnails to switch between images
  • Progress indicator shows annotation status
  • Keyboard arrows also navigate between images
  • Context menu for image-specific operations
Interface Tip: If the interface looks crowded, you can hide/show panels using the View menu or keyboard shortcuts (Tab key toggles tool visibility).

Chapter 3: Creating Your First Annotation (Step-by-Step)

Now for the main event - creating precise medical annotations. We'll annotate a nerve structure step by step:

  1. 1
    Locate the Anatomical Structure
    Examine your ultrasound image and identify the nerve or structure you want to annotate. Use the zoom tool if needed to see fine details clearly. Look for the characteristic hyperechoic or hypoechoic patterns typical of nerve tissue.
  2. 2
    Select the Polygon Tool
    Click the polygon tool icon in the left toolbar (it looks like a connected shape outline) or press the 'P' key. Your cursor will change to a crosshair, indicating you're ready to start drawing.
  3. 3
    Start Tracing the Boundary
    Position your cursor at the edge of the nerve structure and click once to place your first point. A small dot will appear, marking the beginning of your annotation polygon.
  4. 4
    Continue Around the Perimeter
    Move your cursor around the edge of the structure and click to place additional points. Each click adds a vertex to your polygon. Aim for:
    • 10-20 points for typical nerve structures
    • More points for irregular shapes
    • Fewer points for simple, round structures
    • Focus on capturing key anatomical boundaries
  5. 5
    Complete the Shape
    When you've traced around the entire structure, return close to your starting point. Either double-click your final point or press the Enter key to complete the polygon. The shape will automatically close and fill with a semi-transparent color.
  6. 6
    Review and Adjust
    The annotation should now outline your structure. If adjustments are needed:
    • Click the selection tool and select your annotation
    • Drag the corner handles to reshape
    • Press Delete to remove and start over if needed
  7. 7
    Add Medical Attributes
    A properties panel will appear (or right-click the annotation). Fill in the medical details:
    • Structure Type: Select from dropdown (median nerve, ulnar nerve, etc.)
    • Body Side: Choose Left or Right
    • Visibility Quality: Rate as Poor, Fair, Good, or Excellent
    • Patient Position: Supine, lateral, etc.
    • Depth: Approximate depth from skin surface
    • Clinical Notes: Any relevant observations
  8. 8
    Save the Annotation
    Click "Save Current Annotation" button or use Ctrl+S. The annotation is now permanently stored in your session. You'll see a confirmation message and the annotation counter will update.
Expert Technique: For nerve structures with unclear boundaries, focus on the most visible portions and use clinical notes to indicate areas of uncertainty. This provides valuable context for AI training.
Important: Always save your work frequently! While the system auto-saves progress, manual saves ensure your annotations are securely stored.

Chapter 4: Managing Multi-Image Sessions

Most annotation sessions involve multiple images. Here's how to work efficiently across your image set:

Navigation Between Images

  • Thumbnail Strip: Click any thumbnail at the bottom to switch images
  • Keyboard: Use Left/Right arrow keys to move between images
  • Quick Jump: Type an image number to jump directly
  • Progress Tracking: Green dots show completed annotations

Session Progress Tracking

  • Session header shows "Image X of Y" status
  • Progress bar indicates completion percentage
  • Annotation counter shows total annotations created
  • Time tracker records session duration

Workflow Optimization

  • Batch Processing: Complete all images of one type first
  • Quality Check: Review previous images before finishing
  • Consistent Labeling: Use the same naming conventions
  • Break Planning: Save and take breaks every 20-30 minutes

Session Notes

  • Add overall session notes for context
  • Record any technical issues encountered
  • Note image quality variations
  • Document any anatomical variants observed

Chapter 5: Keyboard Shortcuts for Speed & Efficiency

Master these keyboard shortcuts to dramatically improve your annotation speed:

Enter Complete current polygon
Esc Cancel current polygon / Close modals
Space End current session
Delete Delete selected annotation
Ctrl + Z Undo last action
Ctrl + Y Redo last action
Ctrl + S Save current annotation
Ctrl + U Upload new image
Speed Tip: Practice the P → Click → Click → Enter → Ctrl+S sequence until it becomes muscle memory. This basic annotation flow will become lightning fast with practice.

Chapter 6: Saving, Managing, and Exporting Your Work

Proper data management ensures your annotations are safe and accessible. Follow these procedures:

  1. 1
    During Your Session - Save Frequently
    Use Ctrl+S or the "Save Current Annotation" button after each annotation. The system shows a brief confirmation message. Your work is automatically backed up every 5 minutes, but manual saves are more reliable.
  2. 2
    Finishing Your Session
    Before ending your session:
    • Review all images to ensure nothing was missed
    • Click "Export Session Data" to create a backup
    • Add any final session notes
    • Click "End Session" to finalize and store in library
  3. 3
    Accessing Your Library
    Click "Library" in the sidebar to view all your completed sessions. Each session shows:
    • Session date and duration
    • Number of images and annotations
    • Doctor name and category
    • Action buttons (View, Export, Delete)
  4. 4
    Exporting Data
    From the Library page, click "Export" next to any session:
    • JSON Format: Complete data including images (recommended)
    • CSV Format: Annotation coordinates and attributes only
    • COCO Format: Industry standard for machine learning
    • Custom Format: Specify your own export parameters
  5. 5
    Backup Strategy
    Implement a backup routine:
    • Export important sessions immediately after completion
    • Store exports in multiple locations (local drive, cloud, external drive)
    • Weekly backup of the entire application database
    • Monthly archive of exported sessions by date
🔒 Data Security: All data is stored locally on your computer. While this ensures privacy, it also means YOU are responsible for backups. Deleted sessions cannot be recovered.

Chapter 7: Best Practices for High-Quality Annotations

Follow these professional guidelines to create annotations suitable for medical AI training:

Annotation Accuracy

  • Boundary Precision: Trace nerve boundaries precisely, don't approximate
  • Consistent Criteria: Use the same visibility standards throughout
  • Complete Coverage: Annotate all visible relevant structures
  • Avoid Over-annotation: Don't mark uncertain or barely visible structures

📋 Clinical Documentation

  • Detailed Attributes: Fill in all relevant medical fields
  • Consistent Naming: Use standardized anatomical terminology
  • Quality Assessment: Honestly rate image visibility
  • Clinical Context: Include relevant patient positioning and probe details

Quality Control

  • Self-Review: Check your work before saving sessions
  • Peer Review: Have colleagues review complex cases
  • Consistency Checks: Compare similar structures across images
  • Error Correction: Fix mistakes promptly when identified

Efficiency Tips

  • Batch Similar Images: Group images by anatomy or procedure
  • Template Attributes: Set default values for repeated attributes
  • Keyboard Mastery: Learn shortcuts for common operations
  • Break Schedule: Take regular breaks to maintain accuracy
Professional Standard: Aim for annotations that would meet publication quality standards. Future AI models will learn from your work, so invest in accuracy and completeness.
